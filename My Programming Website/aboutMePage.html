Hi, I'm Justin Lin! I've lived in Houston for 9 years and attended Dulles High School. I'm currently a student at the University of Texas at Austin majoring in computer science, and I enjoy learning about programming, baking deserts, swimming, and playing volleyball.

I started coding in elementary school through the JavaScript courses on Khan Academy, which sparked my curiousity in CS. As I learned more about JS, I became more familiar with the language, eventually leading me to start making more complex games in JS.

Throughout high school, I continued exploring more about CS, taking AP Computer Science A, where I learned Java, in addition to 14 other AP classes, scoring a 5 in 13 AP exams. In addition, I joined my high school's Computer Science Club and competed in online and in-person UIL competitions.

In my junior year of high school, I enrolled in a summer course on Machine Learning in Python at Brown University, introducing me to the fields of AI and neural networks. Using the lecture notes from the course and additional information online, I created my letter recognition neural network in my senior year entirely from scratch in Java as part of my Automatic Wordsearch Solver project. Besides Java, I also started exploring Unity and C# through online tutorials.

Although I had learned some of the basics of HTML and CSS in my sophomore year, I eventually started exploring deeper in my senior year, where I used what I learned, such as JS integration and other intricacies to create this website from scratch.

I'm currently interested in learning more about CS, either learning more languages such as SQL or C++ or exploring other projects such as another neural network project, a custom Chrome extension, or an IOS/Android app. I always enjoy learning more, and I'm excited to continue coding!
